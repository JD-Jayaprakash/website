Research Statement


Modulating neural activity in motor and premotor cortices enables external devices to be controlled directly from intention-related signals. Building on early demonstrations from brain-computer interfaces, such as intracortical arrays used to decode motor intent and drive robotic limbs, precise recordings from collections of neurons can restore volitional movement in individuals with paralysis. As implantable prosthetics move toward greater sensory and motor integration, bidirectional interfaces capable of both decoding outgoing signals and delivering targeted sensory feedback represent the next step toward naturalistic control. I intend to research how increasing electrode density, improving signal stability, and refining stimulation protocols can enhance the responsiveness and embodiment of neural-controlled implantable prosthetics.
